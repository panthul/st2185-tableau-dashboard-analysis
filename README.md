##  Project Overview

This project investigates **profitability performance in the Asia-Pacific (APAC) region**, with a deeper focus on **Southeast Asia (SEA)** using Tableau dashboards.

Although APAC generates the **highest revenue among the top regions**, the profit margins remain comparatively weaker. This project explores the drivers behind this gap and identifies operational factors contributing to **structural losses in certain product categories**.

The analysis was conducted through a **multi-level dashboard investigation**, combining regional comparisons, product category performance, and operational factors such as discounts and shipping costs.

---

##  Key Objectives

* Analyse **regional sales vs profitability performance**
* Identify **countries and cities with persistent losses**
* Investigate whether **shipping costs or logistics explain profit leakage**
* Evaluate the impact of **discount strategies on profitability**
* Propose **data-driven recommendations to restore profit margins**

---

##  Key Findings

**1. APAC Revenue vs Profitability**

APAC generates approximately **$3.58M in sales**, making it the strongest revenue contributor. However, strong revenue does not necessarily translate into strong profitability, suggesting inefficiencies in pricing or cost structure.

---

**2. Structural Losses in Southeast Asia**

Within Southeast Asia, certain product categories consistently generate losses.
The **Furniture category — particularly Tables — emerges as the primary driver of negative margins.**

---

**3. Logistics Factors Are Not the Main Driver**

Shipping cost, ship mode, and order priority were analysed but **do not explain the losses**. Negative margins persist regardless of logistics configuration.

---

**4. Discount Strategy as the Primary Cause**

High discount levels significantly reduce margins.
Across segments, products with **higher discount intensity show a strong correlation with negative profits.**

---

##  Recommendation

The analysis suggests that profitability can be restored by **adjusting the discount strategy**, particularly for products in the Furniture category.

Simulation analysis indicates that **moderating discounts could improve margins while maintaining competitive sales performance.**
